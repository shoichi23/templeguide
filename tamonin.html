Tamon-in Temple

Tamon-in temple is dedicated to Jizo Bodhisattva. Tamon-in temple (officially Kongo-san Higan-ji) was founded in 1628 by Jyutsuyo-shonin, originally located in Tsunohazu village in Western Shinjuku. In 1955, this temple was moved to Karasuyama because of urbanization of Shinjuku. Buddhists of this temple devoted themselves to secure people during the famine in the Tempo period. They noted every victim and gave them Buddhist names.

Source from www.tesshow.jp.

Tamonin temple has three stone stasues of Enmei Jizo, wishes people's longevity, Monju Bosatsu, or Gentle Glory, associated with wisdom and Jibo Kannon Bosatsu, or Maria Kannon statue of hidden Christians.

There is also a stone sculpture of Nehanzu, reclining Buddha, brought from Tsubosaka-Ji in Nara prefecture. It is 2 meters tall and 3 meters wide, made of precious stone from the Deccan Plateau. It explicits death of Buddha, his pass from earthly life to the ultimate goal. Being released from the bonds of existence through negation of desires that cause life's intrinsic suffering.
